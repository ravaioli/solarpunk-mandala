# Material Intelligence Framework

Material Intelligence refers to the wisdom of selecting and working with materials that respond intelligently to local environmental conditions while serving multiple functions simultaneously. This intelligence is critical for creating boundaries that are permeable enough to allow circulation but defined enough to maintain integrity.

## Implementation Principles
1. **Consciousness First Principle:** Materials are not dead matter but expressions of MAL's intelligence through local adaptation. Select materials that remember their origin in Mind at Large and express place-specific wisdom.
2. **Local Abundance First:** Prioritize materials that are locally abundant, regenerative, or waste-streams from other processes
3. **Multi-Functionality:** Select materials that serve multiple purposes (structural, thermal, aesthetic, productive)
4. **Adaptive Capacity:** Choose materials that can be seasonally adapted or rapidly reconfigured during crises
5. **Skill Accessibility:** Ensure boundary materials can be worked with locally available skills and tools
6. **Permeability Calibration:** Design boundaries with precisely calibrated permeability based on community needs and dialectical phase
