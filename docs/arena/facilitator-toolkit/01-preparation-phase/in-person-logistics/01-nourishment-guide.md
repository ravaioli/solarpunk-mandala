# Nourishment Guide: Fueling Collaborative Imagination

## The Solarpunk Table

In a solarpunk future, how we meet is as important as why we meet. Our gatherings must be regenerative, not extractiveâ€”and this begins with how we fuel ourselves. The right nourishment acts as a quiet facilitator: stabilizing energy, supporting focus, and embodying the ethics of care and ecological reciprocity we wish to build.

This guide provides practical, low-waste, and energy-sustaining food strategies for in-person sessions. **Feeding people well is a foundational act of facilitation.**

## Guiding Principles

1.  **Sustained Energy:** Prioritize complex carbohydrates, healthy fats, fiber, and protein to avoid energy spikes and crashes.
2.  **Inclusive & Safe:** Proactively account for common dietary restrictions (vegan, gluten-free, nut allergies). Always label food clearly.
3.  **Low-Waste & Local:** Minimize single-use packaging. Choose bulk items and reusable servingware. Source locally when possible.
4.  **Mindful & Unobtrusive:** Food should be easy to eat, quiet, and not disrupt the flow of conversation or deep work.
5.  **Participatory & Joyful:** Whenever possible, make the sharing of food a collaborative, connective ritual.

## Practical Layouts for Session Lengths

### Half-Day Workshop (3-4 hours)
*   **Arrival:** Coffee/tea, water, a simple fruit bowl.
*   **Mid-point Break:** A "nourishment station" with veggie sticks, hummus, whole-grain crackers, and a bowl of mixed nuts/seeds.

### Full-Day Workshop (6-8 hours)
*   **Arrival:** As above.
*   **Morning Break:** Fruit, nuts, perhaps savory muffins.
*   **Lunch:** **The keystone.** Opt for a build-your-own bowl bar:
    *   Bases: Quinoa, brown rice, mixed greens.
    *   Proteins: Lentils, chickpeas, grilled tofu or tempeh.
    *   Veggies: Both roasted and raw options.
    *   Toppings: Seeds, dressings, herbs.
    *   This is inclusive, energy-sustaining, and reduces waste.
*   **Afternoon Break:** "Revival station" with herbal teas, sliced apples with seed butter, and a small offering of dark chocolate (>70%).

### Multi-Day Gathering
*   Rotate and vary options to avoid monotony.
*   Incorporate a **participatory potluck meal** to build community and share culinary culture.
*   For evening socials, consider homemade popcorn with nutritional yeast or a simple, local dessert.

## Facilitator's Checklist: From Plan to Cleanup

*   [ ] **Budget & Source:** Allocate funds. Partner with local bakeries, farms, or co-ops. Buying in bulk reduces cost and waste.
*   [ ] **Communicate:** In pre-session info, ask for dietary restrictions. Plan your menu accordingly.
*   [ ] **Setup & Flow:** Place food in a separate area from the main discussion space to encourage mental breaks.
*   [ ] **The "Hunger Check-in":** As a facilitator, explicitly name breaks as "a time to nourish yourselves." Watch for energy lagsâ€”an unscheduled 10-minute snack break can be a powerful tool.
*   [ ] **Cleanup as Care:** Build cleanup into the agenda. Have clearly labeled compost, recycling, and trash bins. Cleaning together is a communal, grounding task.
