## I. What Is Satsang?

Satsang is not a meeting. It is not a workshop, a ritual, or a strategy session.  
Satsang is the Loom—the living, dynamic process through which the SolarPunk Mandala, the Rhizomatic Arena, the Compass, and the Regenerative Actions are woven into a single, coherent act of co-creation.

The word satsang (Sanskrit: सत्संग) means "gathering in truth" or "community in right relationship." In this context, it is reclaimed not as a spiritual retreat, but as a regenerative praxis: a space where strategy, spirituality, ecology, and economics are held together as one unbroken whole.

These components are hosted on the SolarPunk Sangha platform (solarpunksangha.com). The Community Praxis is a wiki-based community archive. Pods are self-organizing project groups with Resource Kits available for download.

## II. The Four Threads of the Loom

Satsang weaves together four foundational threads—each drawn from your living system:

| The Mandala | Onto-Epistemic Core | Provides the pattern—the sacred geometry of transformation, from –1D Ground to 4D Wholeness. It answers: What is real? How do we know? |
|---|---|---|
| The Arena | Rhizomatic Network | Provides the loom frame—the spatial-systemic field (Ekistics + Circles) where action unfolds. It answers: Where are we? What systems hold us? |
| The Compass | Personal Vocation Tool | Provides the weaver's hand—the individual's unique contribution (Pathway + Vertex). It answers: Who am I here? What is my gift? |
| Regenerative Actions | Strategic Catalog | Provides the colored threads—concrete, dialectical strategies (Liberate ↔ Oppress) grounded in place and power. It answers: What shall we do—and how will we resist co-optation? |

When these four are held together, strategy becomes sacred, and sanctity becomes strategic.

## III. The Weaving Process: Six Phases of Satsang

Satsang unfolds in a non-linear but intentional cycle—a regenerative feedback loop that moves from reflection to action and back again.

### Phase 1: Gather in Truth (Diagnose the Game)
Use Protocol 1 to score the community on the Four Axes
- **Practice**: Collective mapping of the current meta-narrative (Necrocene vs. Symbiotic Commonwealth) using the Meta-Narrative Matrix.
- **Tool**: Rhizomatic Ecosystem Map + Meta-Narrative Strategic Actions.
- Assess community's embodied foundations health
- Map community's dialectical phase and velocity
- **Questions**:
  - "Where is Power Hoarding masquerading as 'efficiency' or 'security'?"
  - "Where are we in contraction? Where is our strength?"
  - "Where are our embodied foundations strong or weak?"
  - "What transformation phase are we in, and what's our movement velocity?"

### Phase 2: Discern the Pattern (Assess Regenerative Force)
Identify leverage points within the 24 Faces
- **Practice**: Scoring the community on the Four Axes (Soteriological, Axiological, Relational, Temporal).

  - Assess community's embodied foundations health
  - Map community's dialectical phase and velocity

### Phase 3: Choose the Pathway (Select a Game-Changing Strategy)
Select the Mandala Pathway that addresses the deepest fracture

- **Tool**: Regenerative Action Catalog (filtered by Pathway + Subdomain).
- **Questions**:
  - "Which thread must we pull to unravel the knot of hoarding?"

- **Tool**: Pod Resource Kits + Digital Dashboards.

### Phase 6: Return to the Loom (Feedback and Renewal)
- **Practice**: Feeding outcomes back into the Mandala's Four Axes, updating the Arena Map, and redrawing the Compass.
- **Tool**: Pod Key Artifacts (e.g., "Annual State of Wellbeing").

## IV. The Loom in Form: Physical and Digital Embodiment

Satsang is not abstract—it is materially instantiated through intentional design:

### Physical Manifestations

1. **The Community Loom**: A large-scale, interactive weaving installation in a central gathering space.
   - **Threads = Actions**: Each Regenerative Action is a colored thread added when implemented.
   - **Tension = Accountability**: Slack or broken threads signal where support is needed.
   - **Pattern = α-coefficient**: A dense, balanced weave reflects high coherence; gaps reveal contraction.

2. **The Pod Altars**: Each Pod maintains a physical "altar" with its Resource Kit (e.g., Mediation Guild's talking piece; Wellbeing Weavers' Care Coordination Kit).

3. **The Honeycomb Council Space**: A physical hexagonal meeting space that embodies boundary permeability through lattice walls that can open and close based on community needs.

### Digital Manifestations

1. **The Loom Dashboard**: A real-time visualization of all active threads across the 24 Faces (Ekistics × Circles). Each thread glows brighter as more community members engage with the action.

2. **The Compass Portal**: An interactive version of the SolarPunk Compass that connects users to their Vocation Vertex and relevant Pod, with seasonal updates to honor temporal rhythms.

3. **The Action Feed**: A live stream of Regenerative Actions being implemented, with photos, stories, and metrics that show tangible impacts.

4. **The Memory Bank**: An AI-enabled knowledge repository that automatically extracts insights from community conversations and documents, creating a living archive that grows wiser over time.

## V. Why Call It Satsang?

The term satsang is chosen deliberately:

1. **It honors wisdom traditions** without appropriating them—framed as gathering in truth, not dogma.

2. **It transcends "workshop" language**, which implies extraction and output. Satsang is about presence and co-creation.

3. **It centers relationality**: Satsang is not about doing to the world, but being with it. It recognizes that transformation happens in the space between individuals, not within isolated heroic acts.

4. **It embodies the Mandala's core insight**: Truth is not found in isolation, but in the space between—where consciousness recognizes itself through relationship.

5. **It creates sacred space for strategy**: The word carries an inherent invitation to slow down, breathe, and listen deeply—counterbalancing the urgency of social and ecological crises with the wisdom of deep time.

In Satsang, we do not "solve" problems.  
We reweave the fabric of belonging—one thread at a time.

## Future Iterations

### 1. The Protocol Suite: Operationalizing the Four Pathways  
*(From Pathways to Actionable Governance)*

**What it is**:  
A set of four core protocols directly tied to your Meta-Narratives "Operational Mechanisms" (e.g., Guided Activity, Appropriate Technology), but structured as Yoga-based governance rituals. Each protocol answers: "How do we govern this domain as a spiritual practice?"

**Why it extends the Tesseract**:  
1. The Four Pathways are currently diagnostic (Compass) and descriptive (Mandala). The Protocols make them prescriptive.
2. They embody the α-coefficient: Each protocol includes metrics for all Four Axes (Soteriological, Axiological, Relational, Temporal).

**The Four Protocols**:

| Pathway | Protocol Name | Meta-Narrative Alignment | Tesseract Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awakening | Conscientização Protocol | Conscious Realism → Materialist Reductionism | Opens Cube 8 (Meta-Perspective) via seasonal rituals that shatter dissociation. |
| Making | Capacitação Protocol | Appropriate Technology → Technocognition | Repairs Cube 5 (Dissociation Boundary) by training embodied skills that reconnect mind/body. |
| Liberation | Liberação Protocol | Ahimsa → Structural Violence | Heals Cube 7 (Systemic Emergence) through participatory budgeting that redistributes power. |
| Healing | Cura Protocol | Guided Activity → Steering Activity | Restores Cube 6 (Intersubjective Gateway) via truth-telling circles that replace punishment with repair. |

**How it works**:  
Each protocol includes:
- **Trigger**: When to activate it (e.g., Liberação Protocol launches when inequality metrics exceed thresholds).
- **Process**: Steps for community facilitation (e.g., Cura Protocol uses "harm mapping" before reconciliation).
- **Artifact**: Tangible output (e.g., Capacitação Protocol produces "Skill Badges" for community credentialing).

*"The Arena defines the stakes. The Protocols define the play."*

### 2. The Living Archive: Embodied Memory Across Time  
*(Honoring the Temporal Axis & –1D Ground)*

**What it is**:  
A physical-digital repository that captures the wisdom of practice—not just documents, but artifacts of transformation (e.g., a repaired tool from a Capacitação workshop, a loom thread from a Cura circle). Inspired by your Meta-Narrative's "Regenerative Temporality" and "Memory & Projection."

**Why it extends the Tesseract**:  
1. The Tesseract's –1D Ground (unmanifest potential) is abstract. The Archive makes it tangible by storing "seeds" of future possibility (e.g., a child's drawing from a Conscientização ritual).
2. It grounds the Temporal Axis: Archives are organized not chronologically, but by dimensional depth (0D survival → 4D wholeness).

**How it works**:  
1. **Physical**: A community "sanctum" with:
   - **Regeneration Reliquaries**: Sealed containers holding soil from healed land, seeds from restored crops.
   - **Story Stones**: Engraved rocks with anonymized narratives of resilience (e.g., "This stone holds the story of a conflict transformed in 2027").
   - **Seasonal Calendars**: Physical artifacts that track ecological and cultural cycles, connecting human rhythms to planetary time.

2. **Digital**: An "Echo Chamber" where AI analyzes artifacts to reveal pattern recognition (e.g., "Communities that practice Liberação Protocol 3x/year show 40% higher α-coherence").
   - **Voice Recordings**: Capturing songs, stories, and ceremonies in their authentic form
   - **Pattern Recognition**: Algorithms that identify recurring themes across different communities and times
   - **Temporal Mapping**: Visualizations that show the evolution of community wisdom across generations

3. **Ritual**: Annual "Seeding Ceremony" where new artifacts are added, and old ones are "replanted" (e.g., composting a broken tool to nourish soil).
   - **First Harvest**: Communities bring their first yields to share wisdom and resources
   - **Ancestor Day**: Honoring those who came before through storytelling and song
   - **Future Letter**: Writing messages to be opened by future generations

*"The Mandala is geometry. The Archive is its memory."*

### 3. The Boundary Medicine School: Training Weavers of Permeability

**What it is**:  
A dedicated training ground for practitioners who specialize in healing dissociation boundaries—those who can sense when boundaries need to open, close, or transform. Drawing from the work of boundary medicine practitioners across traditions, this school trains individuals to become skilled weavers of the social fabric.

**Core Curriculum**:
- **Material Intelligence**: Learning from local ecosystems how boundaries function in nature (honeycombs, mycelial networks, watersheds)
- **Dissociation Recognition**: Identifying when boundaries have hardened into walls of separation
- **Permeability Calibration**: Practices for determining the appropriate level of boundary permeability for each context
- **Ritual Technology**: Designing ceremonies that dissolve artificial separations while maintaining necessary distinctions

**Why It Matters**:  
In a world of either rigid boundaries or boundaryless chaos, the Boundary Medicine School offers a third way: boundaries as organs of relationship that breathe and adapt. This training is essential for those who wish to facilitate healing in fractured communities, polarized societies, and disconnected ecologies.

### 4. The Symbiotic Economy: Circulating Value Without Extraction

**What it is**:  
A practical implementation of the Commons Strategy economic model that replaces extraction with circulation. This is not just an alternative currency or time bank, but a complete reimagining of value creation, distribution, and regeneration.

**Key Features**:
- **Value Ecology Mapping**: Visualizing how different forms of value (material, social, ecological, spiritual) flow through community systems
- **Circulation Metrics**: Tracking not just how much value is created, but how well it circulates (measuring hoarding vs. circulation)
- **Skill-Sharing Infrastructure**: Digital and physical platforms that make it easy to share skills rather than commodify them
- **Resource Stewardship Agreements**: Community-designed frameworks for sharing land, tools, and infrastructure without ownership

**Implementation Example**:  
The "Nutrient Exchange" system in a bioregional community might track not just material flows (food, tools, energy) but also social nutrients (care, attention, wisdom) and ecological nutrients (biodiversity, soil health, water quality), creating a holistic understanding of community health.

*"In the Symbiotic Economy, value is not extracted but circulated. Wealth is not accumulated but regenerated."*
