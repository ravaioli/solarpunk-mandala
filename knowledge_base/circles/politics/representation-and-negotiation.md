## Roles
1. **Capacity Ally** - Works one-on-one or with small groups to help individuals identify their unique gifts and connect them to community needs, fostering agency.
2. **Invitation Weaver** - Actively seeks out and creates pathways for marginalized or quiet voices to participate fully in community life and decision-making.
3. **Participatory Process Designer** - Creates and facilitates engaging, efficient methods for collective decision-making that go beyond mere majority vote.
4. **Bridge Builder** - Ensures all stakeholders, especially those affected by a decision, are consulted and have direct access to the people and processes involved.
5. **Weaving Host** - Fosters a culture of generosity and good faith by organizing shared meals, celebrations, and spaces for informal connection.
6. **Appeals & Grievance Companion** - Guides community members through formal processes to contest decisions, ensuring their standing is recognized and their case heard fairly.

## Commons
- **Most Important Commons:** Deliberative Assembly & Voice Commons
- **Commons Description:** A dynamic, transparent platform that forms the operational heart of the community's participatory democracy. It is designed to ensure every voice can be heard and integrated.
- **Commons Significance:** This Commons is the tangible manifestation of "Democracy and Liberty." It prevents backroom deals by making all negotiation transparent. It is the essential tool for the Bridge Builder to ensure access, the Invitation Weaver to foster inclusion, and the Appeals & Grievance Companion to track disputes. It turns representation from a passive election into an active, ongoing process.

## Protocols
- **Critical Protocol Name:** "Stakeholder Consent & Integration" Protocol
- **Protocol Purpose:** To manage the high-stakes process of making a major community decision that significantly impacts different groups in unequal ways.
- **Protocol Process:** 
  1. Stakeholder Mapping & Initiation: Identifying all affected individuals and groups
  2. Structured Deliberation: Multi-stage process beginning in small groups
  3. Modified Consent Process: Focus on achieving consent from all stakeholder groups
  4. Integration of Objections: Focused negotiation to integrate concerns
  5. Ratification or Return: Revised proposal presented for final consent
- **Protocol Implementation Toolkits:** "Stakeholder Consent & Integration" Protocol Suite with Stakeholder Identification Matrix, Multi-Stage Deliberation Planner, Modified Consent Process Navigator, Objection Integration Mediator, and Ratification Ceremony Designer.
- **Protocol Software:** Representation Optimization Toolkit including Participation Equity Analyzer, Agency Development Planner, Inclusion Barrier Identifier, and Representative Rotation Scheduler.
