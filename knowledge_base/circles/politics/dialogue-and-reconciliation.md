## Roles
1. **Ceremonial Acknowledger** - Designs and holds rituals to mark transitions, celebrate contributions, and formally recognize milestones and resolutions.
2. **Truth & Reconciliation Archivist** - Holds space for the community to confront difficult truths about its past, preserving records to ensure history is not erased.
3. **Dialogue Mediator** - A neutral third party who helps individuals or groups in conflict communicate, understand each other, and find a path forward.
4. **Trust Weaver** - Focuses on repairing and strengthening the fabric of social trust through shared projects and vulnerability-building exercises.
5. **Legacy Keeper** - Helps the community remember its history, learn from its failures, and create pathways for individuals to make amends and be reintegrated.
6. **Radical Welcomer** - The first point of contact for newcomers, ensuring they are greeted with generosity and helped to find their place in the community.

## Protocols
- **Critical Protocol Name:** "Breach of Trust" Reconciliation Protocol
- **Protocol Purpose:** To guide the community through the most high-stakes situation: a major breach of trust (e.g., a betrayal of community principles, abuse of power, or a deep social fracture).
- **Protocol Process:** 
  1. Safe Container Building: Creating psychologically safe environment
  2. Truth-Telling Council: Structured process for all perspectives to be shared
  3. Meaning-Making & Acknowledgment: Synthesizing shared understanding
  4. Co-Creating Redemption: Designing a "Redemptive Action Plan"
  5. Reintegration Ritual: Community-wide ritual to mark closure and reintegration
- **Protocol Implementation Toolkits:** "Breach of Trust" Reconciliation Navigator with step-by-step digital guide, emotional safety checkpoints, Redemptive Action Plan Co-creator, Reintegration Ceremony Planner, and progress tracking.
- **Protocol Software:** Cultural Conflict Resolution Matrix including Context Mapping Tool, Dialogue Structure Builder, Empathy Bridge Generator, and Symbolic Action Library.
