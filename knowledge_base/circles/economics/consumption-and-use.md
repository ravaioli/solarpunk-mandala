## Roles
**Cradle-to-Cradle Systems Designer** - Plans and manages systems where all "waste" is redesigned as nutrient for another biological or technical process.  
**Food System Cultivator** - Grows, forages, or prepares food with a deep understanding of its nutritional, cultural, and ecological significance.  
**Needs & Abundance Mapper** - Creates a public, real-time map of community needs and available resources to optimize matching and minimize waste.  
**Public Utility Maintainer** - Ensures the resilient and equitable operation of decentralized, renewable energy grids and water catchment/purification systems.  
**Post-Extraction Material Guardian** - Manages the responsible and minimal use of legacy industrial materials, prioritizing their conservation in a circular economy.  
**Knowledge Sharer** - Focuses on spreading useful information, skills, and innovations through popular education, rather than creating demand for unneeded goods.

## Commons
**Circular Flow Hub**  
A dynamic, public platform that maps, manages, and optimizes the entire lifecycle of all physical objects and resources within the community, from sourcing to repurposing.

**Significance:** This Commons makes the community's material metabolism visible and manageable. It is the primary tool for eliminating waste, ensuring appropriate use and re-use, and transforming consumption from a private act into a collaboratively managed process.

## Critical Protocol
**"New Object Introduction & Lifecycle" Protocol**  
**Purpose:** To govern the high-stakes decision of introducing any new, non-perishable good into the community's material stream. This is the primary mechanism for preventing waste, avoiding toxin accumulation, and ensuring all objects are designed for the circular economy.

**Process:**  
1. Lifecycle Proposal: Submission detailing source materials, expected lifespan, repair plan, and end-of-life pathway
2. "Waste Audit" Review: Assessment of fit within community's metabolic cycles and waste potential
3. Community Need Assessment: Discussion on whether object fulfills genuine need or duplicates existing goods/services
4. Provisional Adoption with Stewardship Bond: Admission with deposit covering eventual recycling/disposal costs
5. Full Integration & Bond Return: Return of bond once object's lifecycle is proven to be circular

**Implementation Toolkits:**  
- "New Object Introduction & Lifecycle" Protocol Suite: Lifecycle proposal builder, waste audit analyzer, community need validator, stewardship bond calculator, circular integration tracker
- Resource Flow Optimization Toolkit: Metabolic balance calculator, waste stream mapper, utility efficiency optimizer, cultural practice evolver

## Key Artifact
**Annual "Metabolism of Things" Report**  
A comprehensive and visually engaging audit that tells the story of what the community consumes, how it uses it, and where it all goes, measuring progress toward a true circular economy.

**Contents:**  
- The Circularity Quotient: Percentage of material flows that are cyclical versus linear
- Resource Wisdom Narratives: Stories of innovative appropriate use and re-use
- Utility & Footprint Analysis: Data on trends in water/electricity consumption and phasedown of petroleum/metals
- Needs Fulfillment Index: Analysis of how effectively material flows meet actual human needs

**Beauty:** This artifact reframes the community's relationship with "stuff." It is a celebration of mastery over material flows, showcasing a community that has learned to live well by valuing what it already has.

**Generation Systems:**  
- Annual "Metabolism of Things" Report System: Circularity quotient calculator, resource wisdom narrative curator, utility & footprint analyzer, needs fulfillment index
