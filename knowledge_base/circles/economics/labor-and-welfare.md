## Roles
**Meaningful Livelihood Guarantor** - Ensures every community member has access to a variety of meaningful tasks that provide purpose, dignity, and a claim on social wealth.  
**Vocation Mentor** - Helps individuals discover their passions and aptitudes, and connects them to roles where they can best contribute to the common good.  
**Participatory Rotator** - Designs systems to ensure necessary but less desirable tasks are shared equitably, and that power and interesting work are distributed widely.  
**Skill Deepening Coach** - Facilitates continuous learning and skill-sharing to enhance the collective capacities and joyful mastery of the community.  
**Workplace Well-being Guardian** - Assesses all tasks for physical and psychological risks, and advocates for and implements ergonomic and humane practices.  
**Care Network Organizer** - Weaves the web of communal support for children, the elderly, and those who are sick or disabled, recognizing care as fundamental economic work.

## Commons
**Contribution & Care Ledger**  
A multi-dimensional, transparent platform that redefines the concept of a "labor market" into a dynamic map of community contribution and need. It is designed to recognize all forms of work, especially care.

**Significance:** This Commons makes the entire economy of care and labor visible and manageable. It prevents the exploitation and invisibility of care work and ensures everyone's basic needs are met as a right of membership.

## Critical Protocol
**"Burden & Vitality Balancing" Protocol**  
**Purpose:** To address the high-stakes, recurring challenge of ensuring that essential but potentially stressful, repetitive, or undesirable work is distributed fairly, and that no one is overburdened to the point of burnout, while also ensuring these tasks get done.

**Process:**  
1. Task Audit & Impact Assessment: Rating all tasks for stress, desirability, and physical/emotional load
2. Transparent Burden Tracking: Anonymous tracking of "high-burden" task distribution with threshold alerts
3. Proactive Rebalancing: Intervention to reduce individual loads and find others to share work
4. Well-being Check-ins: Ensuring support persons and restorative practices for high-burden roles
5. Systemic Redesign Review: Identifying tasks that could be automated, redesigned, or transformed

**Implementation Toolkits:**  
- "Burden & Vitality Balancing" Protocol Suite: Task audit tool, burden tracking system, rebalancing engine, well-being check-in scheduler, systemic redesign analyzer
- Labor Optimization Toolkit: Meaningful work matchmaker, participatory rotation designer, capacity development planner, care gap analyzer

## Key Artifact
**Annual "State of Work & Well-being" Report**  
A holistic assessment that measures the health of the community not by economic output, but by the dignity, meaning, and safety of its work, and the overall welfare of its members.

**Contents:**  
- The "Dignity of Work" Index: Metrics tracking participation rates, equitable distribution of burdens, and community satisfaction
- Care Network Analysis: Data on provision and receipt of care, measuring gap between care needed and provided
- Skills & Mastery Narrative: Stories of collective skill development and how skill deepening solved challenges
- Well-being & Safety Dashboard: Analysis of health incidents, mental health indicators, and access to leisure

**Beauty:** This artifact fundamentally redefines "welfare" from a state of passive receipt to an active state of being well-cared-for within a web of mutual responsibility. It proves that a society's wealth is in the vitality of its people and the dignity it affords to every kind of work.

**Generation Systems:**  
- Annual "State of Work & Well-being" Report System: Dignity of work index, care network analysis, skills & mastery narrative, well-being & safety dashboard
